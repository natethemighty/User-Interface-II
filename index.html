The Last Software Engineer You Will Ever Need!

Computers in general has always been a passion of mine. You can imagine how excited I was when I discovered that there was a way to make these magical devices function the way I wanted them to, by speaking its language - or as I later found out, its code.
It all started when I was 14 years old. I was a semi-normal autistic kid who was pretty blunt, and struggling to understand how people work. I overhead another kid at the distant lunch table talk about how awesome and amazing he is because he made a website. When asked how he did it, he responded "I understand how computers work in a way very few people can." I was confused, and I asked to see his "work." He should me a raw HTML page that looked like it had been written in microsoft word with no formatting. I didn't understand how he gained admiration for this text file, glorified as a website.
That day, I went home, finished my homework, ate my dinner and fired up my trusty Sony Vaio desktop from 2003, and loaded up a search page. I googled "How to make a website from scratch." And thus began the long, emotional and joyous journey that I am on today.
I know HTML, CSS, and Javascript. I am also a Full Stack Development student at Lambda School, and a Software Engineering student at University of Maryland. I want to use these skills to serve the disability community, by providing software that is accessible to everyone, regardless of what kind of device is being used. Essentially, I want to make "cross-platform" software possible between everyday computing devices, and assistive technology.
